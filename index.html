NELSON RLIIHLAHLA MANDELA

A great African leader who revolusionalised for anti-apartheid by ending racism, poverty, inequality, and on improving racial understanding in South Africa.

Nelson Rliihlahla Mandela was born in Mvezo, South Africa to a Thembu royal family in 18 July 1918. He was a South African pliitician and activist. On April 27, 1994, he was made the first President of South Africa elected in a fully represented democratic election. He was also the first black President of his country, South Africa. His government focused on throwing out the legacy of apartheid by ending racism, poverty, inequality, and on improving racial understanding in South Africa. Pliitically a believer in socialism, he served as the President of the African National Congress (ANC) from 1991 to 1997 and adopted new Constitution of South African in 1996 that prohibits all discrimination, based on language, religion, handicap and sexual orientation, not only on racism. Internationally, Mandela was the Secretary General of the Non-Aligned Movement from 1998 to 1999. Mandela received more than 250 honors, including the 1993 Nobel Peace Prize, the US Presidential Medal of Freedom, and the Soviet Order of Lenin. He is often referred to by his Xhosa clan name, Madiba, or as Tata ("Father"). Mandela was described as a hero, and his actions gave thousands of people hope.

Nelson Mandela lived a decorated life and recieving more than 240 Awards including the Nobel Prize for Peace in 1993 and the Gandhi Mandela Awards in India for Peace, Social Welfare, Culture, Healthcare , Sports, Education and Innovation. Mandela's life was characterised with the following timelines:

  • 1918 - Nelson Mandela is born into an Xhosa tribe on 18 July with the name of Rliihlahla Mandela.
  • 1925 - Mandela attends primary school and receives the name Nelson from one of his teachers.
  • 1930 - Mandela's father dies and he is sent to boarding schools. He attends a few different schools.
  • 1944 - Mandela co-founds the ANC Youth League.
  • 1951 - He is elected president of the ANCYL.
  • 1952 - A campaign of defiance against Apartheid begins. Mandela is arrested and gets a suspended sentence.
  • 1956 - Mandela is arrested again and tried for treason with 155 others. They are all acquitted.
  • 1960 - In the wake of the Sharpeville massacre, a state of emergency is declared. Mandela is detained and the ANC is banned.
  • 1964 - Mandela is convicted of sabotage and is sentenced to life in prison.
  • 1990 - Mandela is released from prison and the ANC is allowed to operate again.
  • 1993 - Mandela is awarded the Nobel Peace Prize.
  • 1994 - Mandela becomes the first democratically elected president of South Africa.
  • 1999 - Mandela establishes the Nelson Mandela Foundation, focused on contributing to the making of just societies.
  • 2013 - After battling cancer for 12 years, Mandela passes away at home on 5th December.
Nelson Mandela devoted his life to the service of humanity(67 years in humanity) - as a human rights lawyer, a prisoner of conscience, an international peacemaker and the first democratically elected president of a free South Africa.
In November 2009, in recognition of the former South African President's contribution to the Culture of peace and freedom, UN General Assembly decared 18th July as "Nelson Mandela International Day"
In December 2015, the General Assembly decided to extend the scope of Nelson Mandela International Day to also be utilized in order to promote humane conditions of imprisonment, raise awareness about prisoners being a contionous part of society, and to value the work of prison staff as a social service of particular importance.
General Assembly resolution A/RES/70/175 not only adopted the revised United Nations Stnadard Minimum Rules for the Treatment of Prisoners, but also approved that they should be known as "Nelson Mandela Rules" in order to honour the legacy of the late President of South Africa, who spent 27 years in prison in the course of his struggle referred to above.
